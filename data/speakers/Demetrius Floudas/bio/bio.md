# Demetrius Floudas

## Biography

Demetrius A. Floudas is a transnational lawyer, AI governance & policy theorist,
and geopolitical adviser. He serves as Visiting Scholar in AI Governance at Downing
College, University of Cambridge, and is Senior Adviser to the Cambridge Existential
Risk Initiative. He has counselled governments, corporations, and think-tanks on
regulatory aspects of policy and technology, including a full-time stint at the
British Foreign Office. He is currently involved in the European AI Office's Plenary
drafting the Code of Practice for General-Purpose AI, and is a member of the EU.AI.
Working Group for AI Systemic Risks. He has reviewed UNESCO's Draft Guidelines for
the Use of AI Systems in Courts and Tribunals and consulted France's CNIL on AI
public information.

## Key Positions

- Treats advanced AI and potential AGI as a civilisational-level risk comparable
  to Weapons of Mass Destruction
- Advocates for a stringent international AI Control & Non-Proliferation Treaty
  with centralised oversight of frontier-capability development
- Proposes prohibition of AI "thanabots" (chatbots simulating the dead)
- Introduces a taxonomy of AI evolution stages (Prenoëtic through Kainonoëtic)
  to conceptualise when existential risks become acute
- Strongly precautionary: favours tight global control of frontier AI
- Critical of letting AI systems make autonomous decisions over human life or death
