# Allison Gardner MP

## Biography

Allison Gardner is the Labour MP for Stoke-on-Trent South. She is an expert in AI
and Data Ethics with interests in health technology, algorithmic bias, diversity and
inclusion. She previously worked with the NHS as a Senior Scientific Adviser for
Artificial Intelligence and served as a director of Women Leading in AI, working
internationally with governments and the UN to advocate for ethical AI. Before
entering politics, she spent 25+ years as a science teacher, completed a PhD in
Molecular Biology, and lectured at Keele University where she led the Data Science
Degree Apprenticeship programme. She is a co-founder and director of Women Leading
in AI.

## Key Positions

- Sceptical of handing life-and-death decisions to AI without robust governance
- Focuses on algorithmic bias (Gender Shades, COMPAS) and its real-world harm
- Advocates for algorithmic impact assessments, audits, and fairness certification
- Warns about "deskilling" when clinicians become dependent on AI
- Argues "the human in the loop wasn't a meaningful human in the loop"
- Believes regulation can inspire innovation (not just constrain it)
- Works on IEEE P7000/P7003 standards for algorithmic bias
